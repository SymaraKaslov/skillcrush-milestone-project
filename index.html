about me

My coding journey began in 2016. I was 28, living in Houston, and had recently quite my job as a flight attendant. One day I happened upon the "best places to work" addition of a well know magazine in Houston. Close to the top of the list was a place called Poetic, a design and development firm. I navigated to their website and it was there that I first discovered code. One day, I decided that I was going to mozey on up to Poetic and ask them for a job without any prior coding experience. I did just that. Allthough I didnt get hired that day, they were nice enough to point me in the right direction: they sent me on my way with a referal to a coding boot camp in the area. I took up the mandate. Needless to say, boot camp was tuff, and I didnt make it past the first month. Depressed and defeated, I put down code. Over the next two years, I heard whispers from my inner being encouraging me to take up the mandate once more, but in attempt to protect myself from the pain of failure I resisted. However, time heals most wounds. I discovered skillcrush in 2018 and in a truly Symara fasion, I consulted the i ching (a 2000 year old oracle) on the matter. "im interested in taking the break into tech blueprint class at skill crush. It costs $1300 dollars."" I recieved the message "true and confident opening. regrets vanish". I decided to take the risk.
